Detail Removal

Detail removal — reducing the complexity of an algorithm or process by focusing on the important parts

In our PB&J algorithm, we could have used exact measurements (put 3 oz. of PB on the knife)...

But the algorithm works well enough by putting an inexact amount and repeating until spread well.

Generalization

Generalization — combining a group of related concepts or processes into a single category

Spreading PB exact same "code" as spreading jelly...

We could share the same code generically taking a condiment and spreading it!

Procedural Decomposition

Procedural decomposition — breaking a problem down into smaller subtasks, usually to increase readability and/or maintainability, often by applying one of the above concepts

Peanut Butter & Jelly Sandwich:
  1. Get plate, knife, napkin, bag; place onto table
  2. Get bread bag, peanut butter jar, jelly jar; place onto table
  3. Open the bag of bread
  4. Take out 2 slices of bread, put them on the plate large side down
  5. Close the bag of bread
  6. Open the jar of peanut butter
  7. Put the lid on the table
  8. Take the knife and put it in the jar
  9. Scoop some peanut butter onto it
  10. Spread the peanut butter across the large side
  11. Repeat the last 3 steps until evenly spread on the bread
  12. Put the lid back onto the jar
  13. Clean the knife with the napkin
     
  1. Open the jar of jelly
  2. Put the lid on the table
  3. Take the knife and put it in the jar
  4. Scoop some jelly onto it
  5. Spread the jelly across the large side of the other slice of bread
  6. Repeat the last 3 steps until evenly spread on the bread
  7. Put the lid back onto the jar
  8. Clean the knife with the napkin
  9. Place the slices of bread on top of each other so that both the peanut butter and jelly are touching
  10. Open the bag
  11. Slide the sandwich into the bag so that the large side of bread is parallel with the large side of the bag

Lasagna

Lasagna:
  1. Prepare materials
  2. Cook pasta
  3. Repeat 4 times:
    1. Build a layer
  4. Put dish into oven for 40 minutes, then take it out


Prepare materials:
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Bring salted water to boil in a pot
  3. Get a baking dish
  4. Get pasta, tomato sauce, cheese, cooked meat or veggies


Cook pasta:
  1. Put pasta sheets into boiling water for 10 minutes
  2. Drain the water
  3. Rinse the cooked pasta with cool water
     
Build a layer:
  1. Spread tomato sauce evenly
  2. Spread meat or veggie filling evenly
  3. Cover with a pasta sheet
  4. Spread cheese evenly
