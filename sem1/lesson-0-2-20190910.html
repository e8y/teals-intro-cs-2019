First Computers


Katherine Johnson
Can you imagine what the first computer looks like?
Johnson, Vaughan, and Jackson all began their careers at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA)—which later became NASA—working as “computers.” Computers were not what we think of them today. They were people, primarily women, who reduced or analyzed data using mechanical calculators.
Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum
Katherine Johnson calculated the trajectory for America's first human spaceflight (flown by Alan Shephard)

Key Terms

Computer
Program
Algorithm Complete, well-defined set of steps to solve a problem
Al-Khwārizmī (خوارزمی) → Algorithm
Computer: machine that performs calculations; process, store and retrieve data; Program: set of steps in a language the computer can understand and follow; Algorithm: complete, well-defined set of steps to solve a problem

Key Terms

Computer Science
Programming Language
Computer Science: The study of the theory and use of computers; Programming Language: A language (vocabulary and grammar) understood by a computer, which it can follow to perform tasks

Game Plan

Work in table teams to develop an algorithm to direct a human computer (me) to make a PB&J sandwich.

When I say Go...

  1. Pick one person at your table to be the writer
  2. Brainstorm with your team and write an algorithm on paper
  3. After 10 minutes, team(s) will come up to the speaker to read their algorithm
  4. The human computer will perform your team's algorithm!
